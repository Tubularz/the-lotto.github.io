The Event

An evening with the village

Join fellow fans for a rare communal screening of Larry Yust's 1969 short film adaptation of Shirley Jackson's "The Lottery" — followed by a moderated fan discussion exploring why this eighteen-minute film continues to disturb, fascinate, and provoke more than half a century after its release.

The screening will be presented in its original format. No commentary, no interruption — just the film as it was made, in a room full of people who know exactly what is coming and will watch anyway. There is something uniquely unsettling about that experience, and that is precisely the point.

Following the screening, we move into a structured fan discussion: two hours of guided conversation about the story, the film, Shirley Jackson's life and work, and what it all means in the present day. All perspectives welcome. Quiet voices especially encouraged.

Programme

The evening's order of events

On arrival

Doors open & welcome

Guests are welcomed and seated. A brief introduction to the evening sets the tone. The black box is on the table. We do not explain the black box.

The screening

The Lottery (1969) — Larry Yust

The film is presented in full, uninterrupted. Eighteen minutes. Runtime is short. The effect is not.

Interval

A brief pause

Time to collect your thoughts, speak quietly with those seated near you, or simply sit with what you've just seen. Refreshments available.

Fan discussion

Moderated fan discussion — approx. 2 hours

A structured conversation for fans, guided but not controlled. We will work through the film, the story, Shirley Jackson's life and intentions, and the question no one can quite stop asking: why does this still feel so relevant?

Close of evening

Informal close

The structured discussion ends but conversation continues. The evening closes when the last person leaves. There is no lottery at the end. Probably.

"The idea of 'The Lottery' is simply that a group of people can do an irrational, terrible thing and never question it."

Shirley Jackson, in correspondence, 1948

Fan Discussion

After the film

The discussion group is the centrepiece of the evening. It is not a lecture. It is not a seminar. It is a room of people who care deeply about this story, talking honestly about why.

The facilitator will open with a series of questions and then largely get out of the way. Every voice matters. First-time viewers and longtime devotees of Jackson's work are equally welcome — both perspectives enrich the conversation in different ways.

There are no wrong answers. There are only questions we've been sitting with for a long time and haven't had the right room to ask.

Discussion threads

  • Why does the story provoke such visceral discomfort even when we know the ending?
  • What does the film add or change compared to Jackson's original text?
  • The role of women in the lottery's ritual — what does the film do with this?
  • Old Man Warner and the defence of tradition: who do we know like this?
  • Tessie Hutchinson's protest — is it principled, or is it only personal?
  • What does the story say about the communities we live in today?
  • Jackson's life and her complicated relationship with her own community

Discussion group

Fan-led discussion

The evening's discussion is facilitated by a member of the fan community. No academic credentials required. No prior knowledge assumed. Just a genuine interest in the story and why it matters.
